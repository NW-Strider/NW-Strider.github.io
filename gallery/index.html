Bouquet

by Nina Whipple

My final project for MAT 111PF consists of 11 3D printed flowers, several 3D printed TPU "vines", and several laser-cut "squiggles" made of wood. I made these with the intention of using them in another project, "Entangled", as well as the "Sound Garden", both of which can be seen below. These 3D printed items vary in size as well as design, as there are three different flowers and three different wooden shapes. This project was originally unnamed, however, I thought "Bouquet" was fitting as the project turns into a "Garden".

Entangled

A group collaboration for MAT 111FS

An effort to bridge the gap between the natural and unnatural through human connection, Entangled is a physical structure that employs wood, wire, 3D-printed items, as well as interactive lights and sounds. The exhibit was originally installed in the TRANslab of Elings Hall, but is in the process of moving to the Glassbox Gallery on campus. Our work addresses AI and equity by promoting inclusivity and accessibility. he installation invites viewers to engage with both natural and technological elements, reflecting the dynamic relationship between humans and machines. As participants interact, their movements are captured and translated into data using TouchDesigner, controlling pane of flowers under the viewer. This visual feedback loop highlights the mutual influence of human actions and AI, demonstrating how technology can foster deeper connections. The interactive design ensures a wide audience can participate, showcasing AI as a tool that enhances human experiences without exclusion.
